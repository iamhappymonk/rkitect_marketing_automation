# rkitect.ai Company OS

This document is the operating layer for rkitect.ai marketing work. It explains how the team should make decisions, organize work, use AI, and keep brand execution consistent.

## Purpose

rkitect.ai needs a single internal source of truth that helps the team produce consistent marketing, sales, content, outreach, product narrative, and AI-assisted work.

## Operating Principles

- Brand truth is centralized before execution.
- Research, assumptions, and approved claims must be separated.
- Every output should map to an audience, pain point, message, channel, and CTA.
- AI accelerates drafts and workflows, but humans approve strategy, claims, outreach, and publishing.
- Reusable agents and skills must stay brand-agnostic and load rkitect.ai context at runtime.

## Team Cadence

- Weekly: review active tasks, content pipeline, research learnings, and open decisions.
- Monthly: review positioning, GTM priorities, channel performance, and SOP gaps.
- Per campaign: run campaign brief, asset review, launch QA, and retrospective.

## Decision Rules

- Approved facts belong in context and brand files.
- Assumptions belong in open questions or validation logs until confirmed.
- Major changes to positioning, pricing, audience, brand voice, or public claims must be logged.
- Any claim that affects trust, compliance, product performance, or competitors needs proof before public use.

## AI Usage Rules

- AI-generated copy is draft material.
- AI agents must load runtime context from this repo.
- Do not hardcode rkitect.ai details into reusable agent or skill definitions.
- Do not use AI to fabricate testimonials, benchmarks, customer proof, or competitor claims.
- Customer-facing outreach should be human-reviewed before first touch.

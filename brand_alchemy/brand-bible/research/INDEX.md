## 🔄 HOW AGENTS SHOULD NAVIGATE THIS FOLDER

### Agent Creating Social Content

**Workflow:**

1. Open `social-community-intelligence-report.md`
2. Find the pain point or audience segment you're addressing
3. See: which accounts to reply to, which hashtags to use, which Reddit communities to engage
4. Execute on specific angle listed in the research
5. Track metrics (replies, engagement, leads) back to this research

**Example:** If creating content about render time frustration:

- Research shows: 15+ Reddit threads with "Lumion took 30 hours"
- Research recommends: Reply on r/archviz with "here's a hybrid workflow"
- Research suggests: Also reply to @Lumion3d posts with segmentation angle
- Expected result: 3-5 qualified leads per week from this targeting

### Agent Extending Research to New Platform

**Workflow:**

1. Open `RESEARCH-STANDARDS-GUIDE.md`
2. See the "5 Standards" and what rkitect research looks like for each
3. Open `VAGUE-VS-SPECIFIC.md` to verify your specificity level
4. Conduct research on new platform (LinkedIn, YouTube, TikTok, Discord)
5. Follow the rkitect model: 30+ accounts, 40+ hashtags, 20+ communities, pain points, engagement opportunities
6. Save in this folder with clear naming convention

**Example:** Researching LinkedIn for rkitect:

- Use `RESEARCH-STANDARDS-GUIDE.md` to know you need: 20+ company types + specific roles, industry groups, best-performing content types, pain points specific to each role
- Reference rkitect's Twitter research as a model for depth and structure
- Create: `social-community-intelligence-linkedin-report.md` with same level of specificity
- Share with team to guide LinkedIn content strategy

### Agent Reviewing Someone Else's Research

**Workflow:**

1. Open `VAGUE-VS-SPECIFIC.md`
2. Check: Does it answer all 8 questions? (WHAT/WHO/WHY/WHAT THEY WANT/HOW/METRIC/TIME/OUTCOME)
3. Check red flags: Any "many/most/some" without numbers? Any "good engagement" without baseline?
4. Compare structure to rkitect research: Are there specific accounts/hashtags/communities? Or is it generic?
5. Ask: "Can I run with this and execute?" If not, ask for more specificity

**Example:** Reviewing intern's YouTube research:

- Research says: "YouTube has channels about architecture with good engagement"
- Check against standard: ❌ No specific channel names ❌ No video types ❌ No comment analysis
- Reference rkitect's Twitter research model: Should include 20+ specific channels, comment section pain points, upload frequency, etc.
- Send back for revision with pointer to `RESEARCH-STANDARDS-GUIDE.md`

# Voice And Tone

## Voice

The rkitect.ai voice is the trusted colleague: direct, practical, professional, and familiar with architecture studio pressure.

## Tone Rules

- Use concrete workflow language.
- Answer the practical problem first.
- Be transparent when talking about AI.
- Avoid sounding like a generic SaaS landing page.
- Avoid exaggerated claims and vague hype.

## Use Often

- render
- revision
- client-ready
- material option
- scene
- plan
- model
- presentation
- workflow
- visual option

## Avoid

- magical
- revolutionary
- effortless
- perfect
- disrupt
- replace architects
- never render again
- guaranteed

## Channel Tone

| Channel | Tone |
| --- | --- |
| Website | Clear, evidence-led, product-specific |
| Instagram | Visual, fast, concrete, process-led |
| LinkedIn | Business-practical, ROI-aware, studio productivity focused |
| Reddit | Helpful, transparent, low-promotion |
| X/Twitter | Concise, peer-level, value-first |
| Email | Segmented, useful, low-fluff |

## Example Copy

- "The client changed the flooring again. Click the floor, swap the material, keep the meeting moving."
- "Same room. Three finish directions. No full re-render."
- "Use AI for iteration, not fake architecture."

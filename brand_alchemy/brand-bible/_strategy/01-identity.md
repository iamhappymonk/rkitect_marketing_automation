# 01 — Brand Identity

**Purpose:** This document defines WHO rkitect.ai is, what we stand for, and how we show up in the world. Use this to guide all brand expressions.

---

## Brand Name

**rkitect.ai**

Lowercase, no space. Pronunciation: "architect dot A.I." The 'r' represents "real" (real architects, real workflows) and reminds us of our architectural focus.

---

## Essence (The Core)

**Fast, controllable architectural visualization for real design workflows.**

*Why this matters:* This is the north star. Every marketing message, feature, and decision should ladder up to this. If something doesn't align with "fast + controllable + architectural," it's off-brand.

---

## Promise (What We Deliver)

rkitect.ai helps architects and design teams **create and revise client-ready visuals** without:
- Slow render cycles (hours to days)
- Expensive tooling pressure ($1000+ licenses, GPU costs)
- Dedicated visualization teams (outsourcing, overhead)

**Real outcome:** Architects spend time on design, not rendering. Studios improve margins. Students get professional-quality visuals without expensive gear.

---

## Personality Traits

**Trusted Colleague**
- We speak their language (studio culture, client pressure, deadline stress)
- We get it; we're not condescending
- Example: "The client changed the flooring again" ← acknowledges real frustration, not hypothetical

**Studio-Native**
- Designed for how architects actually work (SketchUp → quick concept → client revision → approval)
- Respects existing tools (V-Ray for hero shots, SketchUp for modeling, Revit for BIM)
- Not a replacement; a layer on top

**Direct and Practical**
- We say what we mean
- No buzzwords or vague benefit claims
- Example: "3-5 minute renders from floor plan or sketch" (not "lightning fast")

**Confident Without Hype**
- We know what we're good at
- We're honest about limitations
- We don't oversell or exaggerate
- Example: "AI rendering with control, not hallucination"

**Transparent About What AI Can and Cannot Do**
- We acknowledge AI risks (accuracy, geometry distortion, style errors)
- We position segmentation as a *control layer*, not a magic button
- We show guardrails and limitations alongside benefits

## Known For (Brand Positioning)

### AI Rendering With Control
Not generic AI image generation. We preserve geometry from CAD/BIM/3D imports and allow **per-element editing** without re-rendering.

**Example:** "Swap flooring, lighting, and furniture in a rendered room without waiting for a full re-render. Your model stays accurate."

### Post-Render Element Editing (Segmentation)
Rendered image → clickable elements (walls, flooring, furniture, lighting) → edit without re-rendering.

**Example:** You have a render with 5 furniture options. Instead of rendering 5 times (5 hours), you render once (5 minutes) and use segmentation to test material/color swaps (30 seconds each).

### Fast Visual Options
Move from concept to client-presentable visuals in minutes, not days.

**Example:** Monday morning: architect gets floor plan from client. By Tuesday 10am: 5 design direction renders ready for client presentation (instead of Wednesday or Thursday).

### India-Ready Pricing and Context
- Pricing that works for small Indian studios ($X/month, not $X/year)
- Cultural understanding (Vastu, regional materials, urban density challenges)
- INR payment options, localized support
- Free tier for students and competitions

### Student and Small-Studio Accessibility
- Free 20-render tier for portfolio building
- Student pricing ($X/month vs V-Ray's $300/year)
- Team plans for studios (5-20 people)
- No hardware requirements beyond a laptop

## NOT Known For (What We Actively Avoid)

### ❌ Replacing Architects
We are a tool that helps architects work faster, not an architect replacement. Explicit positioning: "AI for iteration, not design decisions."

### ❌ Generic AI Art
We're not Midjourney or Stable Diffusion. We preserve architectural accuracy and geometry from CAD/BIM inputs.

### ❌ Unrealistic Fantasy Renders
We don't produce "pretty but wrong" visualizations. If the model has a load-bearing wall, the render respects it.

### ❌ Spammy Outreach
No mass DMs, unsolicited replies, or paid ads disguised as organic content. We earn attention through genuine value.

### ❌ Unsupported Claims
No claims about "guaranteed" speed, "perfect" accuracy, or "replaces X tool entirely." We're specific and verified.

## Brand Identity Checklist

**Before publishing ANY marketing content, verify:**

- [ ] Does this content respect architects (not replacing them, not condescending)?
- [ ] Is the tone "trusted colleague" or "generic SaaS hype"?
- [ ] Am I being specific (e.g., "3-5 minutes" not "very fast")?
- [ ] Am I transparent about AI limitations?
- [ ] Does this ladder up to "fast, controllable, architectural"?
- [ ] Would a cynical architect find this credible or dismissive?
- [ ] Have I verified any claims (speed, accuracy, pricing)?

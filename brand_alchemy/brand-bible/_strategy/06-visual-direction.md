# Visual Direction

## Visual Principles

- Show proof before polish.
- Use before/after comparisons.
- Make the workflow visible.
- Highlight control, not just output beauty.
- Use architecture-native inputs: sketches, plans, models, photos, renders.

## Color Direction

Use a restrained, professional palette with strong contrast. Avoid making generic purple/blue AI gradients the dominant brand look.

## Typography Direction

Clear, modern, legible typography. Favor professional confidence over decoration.

## Layout Direction

- Side-by-side comparisons.
- Annotated screenshots.
- Input to output sequences.
- Material option grids.
- Client presentation examples.

## Imagery Direction

- Interior and exterior architecture renders.
- Indian apartments, homes, retail, hospitality, and developer contexts.
- Real workflow screens and visual proof.
- Student portfolio and jury contexts.

## Anti-Patterns

- Abstract AI orbs.
- Fantasy buildings presented as practical architecture.
- Dark blurred stock-like visuals.
- Unsupported product screenshots.
- Overly decorative SaaS pages.

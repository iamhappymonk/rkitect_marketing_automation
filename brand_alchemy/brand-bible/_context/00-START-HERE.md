# \_context/ — Brand Reference Materials

**This is the single source of truth for rkitect.ai brand context.** Load relevant files BEFORE creating any marketing content.

---

## 📍 File Guide

### PRIMARY REFERENCE

**`brand-bible.md`** — Complete brand strategy document

- Brand identity, essence, promise, values, personality
- Positioning statement and market wedge
- Target audiences and detailed customer personas
- Competitive landscape and win/loss notes
- Messaging pillars and objection handling
- Brand voice and on/off-brand copy examples
- Design direction and content guidelines
- Go-to-market strategy and metrics
- **Start here for** any brand question or campaign planning

### SUPPORTING DETAIL DOCUMENTS

**`claims-and-proof.md`** — Verification table for public claims

- Public claims verification status (approved vs needs proof)
- Evidence requirements for each claim
- Which claims are safe to use publicly
- Compliance and registration status
- **Use before** making any public claims about speed, pricing, accuracy, or compliance

**`company-profile.md`** — Company and business model overview

- Company narrative and product promise
- Current audience focus and strategic wedge
- Business model and pricing tiers
- Source inputs and data references
- **Use for** high-level company context and pricing verification

**`customer-context.md`** — Customer pain points and triggers

- Audience language and specific pain points
- Buying triggers and decision drivers
- Customer acquisition opportunities
- **Use for** writing content that resonates with customer language

**`market-context.md`** — India-focused market opportunity

- India-specific themes (Vastu, compact apartments, climate, tier cities)
- Channel benchmarks (architecture media, Instagram, hashtags)
- India hashtags and community focus areas
- Competitive landscape overview
- **Use for** India-market strategy and channel selection

**`product-context.md`** — Product capabilities and use cases

- Documented capabilities and features
- Core use cases (client presentations, last-minute changes, student portfolios)
- Claims needing validation
- **Use for** product feature accuracy and use case messaging

## 🎯 How Agents Should Use This Folder

### Creating Social Content

1. Open `brand-bible.md` → Find relevant message pillar
2. Open `customer-context.md` → See pain point language
3. Open `market-context.md` → Check if India-specific opportunity applies
4. Open `claims-and-proof.md` → Verify any claims are approved
5. Draft content aligned to voice in brand-bible.md

### Writing Campaign Copy

1. Open `brand-bible.md` → Messaging pillars + Go-to-market section
2. Open `company-profile.md` → Confirm product promise and positioning
3. Open `product-context.md` → Verify features mentioned are real
4. Open `claims-and-proof.md` → Cross-check any ROI/speed claims
5. Use on/off-brand copy examples as templates

### Planning Market Strategy

1. Open `brand-bible.md` → Target audiences section
2. Open `market-context.md` → See channels, hashtags, India opportunities
3. Open `customer-context.md` → Understand buying triggers
4. Use to inform channel mix, content themes, timing

### Verifying Brand Alignment

1. Open `brand-bible.md` → Brand voice section + on/off-brand examples
2. Review your content against voice attributes: trusted colleague, direct, practical, confident, transparent
3. Check it doesn't use off-brand language: magical, revolutionary, effortless, replace architects
4. Compare to on-brand examples provided

### Making Public Claims

1. **ALWAYS** open `claims-and-proof.md` first
2. Find your claim in the table
3. Check status: Approved ✓ / Needs Proof ✗ / Needs Verification ?
4. If not marked "Yes" for "Use Publicly?" — do not use
5. If status is "Needs proof/verification" — escalate to leadership

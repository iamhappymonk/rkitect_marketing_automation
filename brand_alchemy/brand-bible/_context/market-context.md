## Competitive Landscape

### Direct Competitors: AI Rendering Tools

**ArchiVinci AI**

- Strengths: Strong sketch-to-render, visual social presence.
- Key weakness: No spatial segmentation; treats image as flat pixels. Failed render or hallucination costs credits you cannot recover (credit anxiety).
- rkitect.ai advantage: Segmentation + predictable cost.

**mnml.ai**

- Strengths: Architecture AI assistant with style variation.
- Key weakness: Credit-based pricing creates "credit anxiety." 250 images/month sounds sufficient until client revision rounds. No path to construction-ready output.
- rkitect.ai advantage: Flat ₹16.50/render rate eliminates credit anxiety entirely. Behavioral unlock.

**MyArchitectAI**

- Strengths: Browser-based, accessible to non-professionals.
- Key weakness: Tech transparency opacity is trust liability with B2B clients. No BIM integration. Limited customization.
- rkitect.ai advantage: Spatial Intelligence + transparent technology stack.

**LookX & Other Credit-Based Tools**

- Key weakness: One-time or credit-based pricing creates dead period between purchases. No incentive to iterate weekly.
- rkitect.ai advantage: Monthly flat rate encourages experimentation.

**Veras (McNeel)**

- Strengths: Integration with Rhino/SketchUp.
- Key weakness: Hard dependency on expensive CAD software. Only works with 3D models; zero value for field architects with phones or hand sketches. $59/mo per seat adds up fast for 5–10 person studios in India.
- rkitect.ai advantage: Works from hand sketches, site photos, WhatsApp images, CAD exports. No software prerequisite.

**Coohom**

- Analysis: Actually a furniture marketplace, not an architecture tool. Real customer is furniture brands, not architects. Coohom actively harms architects with custom designs by trying to replace geometry with catalog items.
- rkitect.ai advantage: Zero overlap in revenue model. Architects are the customer, not an afterthought.

### Legacy Rendering Tools (High-Friction)

- V-Ray: High-fidelity rendering trust, but expensive and workflow-heavy.
- Lumion: Real-time visualization strength, but hardware and render-time friction.
- Enscape: Real-time walkthrough and VR strength, but still often needs polish.
- 3DS Max/AutoCAD: Enterprise rendering, not accessible to small studios or students.

# Customer Context

_Last updated: 2026-05-13_

## Primary Audience Segments (5 High-Value Segments)

### Segment 1: Independent & Small Studio Architects (Primary)

**Profile:** Principals and design leads at 2–25 person architecture firms.
**Pain:** Spending 3–4 days per client pitch on rendering and revisions. Cannot justify hiring a full visualization team.
**Trigger:** Lost a client pitch because renders took too long or looked generic. Freelancer took too long to revise.
**Value:** Speed (turnaround from 4 days to 4 hours), control (edit renders without going back to 3D model), affordability.

### Segment 2: Interior Designers & Boutique Studios

**Profile:** Design directors and freelance interior designers managing residential, hospitality, and retail projects.
**Pain:** Clients keep asking for material and color changes. Each change = re-render cycle with external team or expensive software.
**Trigger:** Late material selection or color brief from client. Crit or presentation tomorrow.
**Value:** Instant material swaps, no waiting for external renderer, predictable monthly cost.

### Segment 3: Real Estate Developers & Builders

**Profile:** Marketing heads and pre-sales teams needing 10–100 renders per project.
**Pain:** Expensive per-render costs when using traditional rendering services. Need fast turnaround for marketing collateral.
**Trigger:** New project launch, pre-sales marketing need, investor presentation.
**Value:** Bulk rendering at predictable cost, fast options for client exploration, can iterate on design messaging quickly.

### Segment 4: Freelance Architects Building Practices

**Profile:** Solo architects and small freelance teams competing with larger studios.
**Pain:** Need enterprise-quality output to justify fees. Limited budget for expensive rendering tools.
**Trigger:** Major project pitch, portfolio strengthening, need to compete with better-funded studios.
**Value:** Professional output without enterprise overhead, affordable monthly plan, can produce multiple options.

### Segment 5: Architecture Students

**Profile:** Thesis, competition, and portfolio builders across Tier-1 and emerging architecture schools.
**Pain:** Need portfolio-ready visuals but cannot afford V-Ray, Lumion, or rendering services. Unlimited experimentation difficult.
**Trigger:** Jury presentation, competition deadline, need to strengthen portfolio.
**Value:** Free tier for basics, affordable Pro tier for serious projects, no software licensing hassle.

## Audience Language

- "Render took 30 hours."
- "Client keeps asking for minor changes."
- "V-Ray/Lumion is too expensive."
- "AI renders look good but I cannot control the details."
- "I spend more time checking AI output than doing it myself."
- "SketchUp to Lumion/V-Ray to Photoshop is too long."
- "Crit tomorrow and my renders look flat."
- "Clients do not understand plans."
- "I need quick concept renders, not final hero shots."

## Primary Pains

- Long render times.
- Expensive traditional rendering tools.
- Weak hardware or license access.
- Client revision fatigue.
- Distrust of AI hallucination.
- Too many tools in the visualization pipeline.
- Students need affordable portfolio-ready visuals.
- Freelancers and small studios need stronger visuals to justify fees.

## Buying Triggers

- Client presentation deadline.
- Pitch deck deadline.
- Late material or furniture change.
- Need to show multiple options.
- Student jury, thesis, or competition.
- Real estate listing or buyer presentation.
- Need to compete with better-funded studios.
- Need to show a client a concept before committing to full production rendering.

## Objections

- AI may distort details.
- Output may be less accurate than traditional rendering.
- Tool may create more checking work.
- Paid AI tools may feel risky for students.
- Professionals may not trust unsupported quality claims.

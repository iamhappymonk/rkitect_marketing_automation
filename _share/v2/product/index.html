How the project runs

Brief. Concept. Schematic. Design development. Render. Revision. Pin-up. Memory.

  1. 01

    Brief intake

    Reads the brief and references, sets up the programme, asks the questions a good associate would.

  2. 02

    Concept / pre-design

    Site context, programme cards, mood and finishes references, early massing options.

  3. 03

    Schematic design

    Floor plans drafted and edited, spaces named and sized, adjacencies, parametric structure from sketch, CAD, or SKP.

  4. 04

    Design development

    3D model from plan, material and lighting and finish specs per space, multi-angle views, hero shots.

  5. 05

    Rendering

    Photoreal renders. ControlNet plus diffusion routing. Multi-style options. Multi-angle decks.

  6. 06

    Revision rounds

    Click any rendered element to swap material, finish, fixture, or object. No full re-render. Background queue for batches.

  7. 07

    Pin-up / client review

    Moodboards, palettes, hero shots, multi-angle decks — ready for the pin-up wall.

  8. 08

    Project memory

    Brief, programme, spaces, finishes, revisions, redlines — carried across sessions. Pick the project up tomorrow without re-briefing.

Revision rounds

Click any rendered element. Swap the material. Render stays.

Rendered output is segmented into the elements an architect would mark up — walls, floors, finishes, fixtures, furniture, lighting. Click any one and swap. The rest of the scene stays put. No mask drawing. No full re-render of the room to change a sofa.

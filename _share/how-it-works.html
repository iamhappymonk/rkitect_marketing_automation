Discovery flow

Three phases before the first render.

Domain-aware intake, gated by project type. Residential, commercial, hospitality and outdoor each get a tailored question set. The studio never starts rendering until it understands the brief.

i

Context

Site diagram drawn on the fly. Type-specific questions surface — orientation, programme, constraints, climate, brief.

Output · Site diagram + your brief, captured in your voice
ii

Precedents

A small set of precedent images, drawn to your brief. Pick one or two — the studio uses them as anchors for the thesis.

Output · 1–2 anchor precedents + a starting palette
iii

Direction

A bubble diagram and parti sketch — the studio's reading of your brief, shown for confirmation or refinement before a single render is spent.

Output · Bubble diagram + parti, ready to refine

Watch the studio work

Every decision, narrated back.

You see the room being comprehended before you see the render. Specialists check in as they hand off. You can interrupt, redirect, or branch at any moment. Step away and come back — the studio kept working; nothing was lost.

Like sitting next to your senior architect.

Not a black box. Not a spinner. Each step shows you what the studio is doing, what it has decided, and where it is going next — written the way a colleague would tell you, not the way a machine would log it.

Rubric review

Forty-two points. A second pass refuses weak briefs.

Every thesis is composed against a 42-point rubric — palette, programme, lighting strategy, precedent justification, materials defensibility. A second pass reviews the result. If it isn't strong enough, the studio refines and tries again before showing it to you.

Memory

It remembers your studio.

Past projects, preferences, vetoes — all carried into the next brief. Your studio's voice compounds across projects instead of resetting every session.

The studio learns your voice over time.

After every project, the studio absorbs what you chose, what you rejected, and which precedents won. Next time you brief it, those decisions are already on the table — proposed before you have to ask.

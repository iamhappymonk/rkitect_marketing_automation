Audit the following website copy and score it out of 100:

$ARGUMENTS

## Scoring Criteria (each out of 10)

1. **Headline impact** — Does it pass the "so what?" test? Is it outcome-driven, not product-name-driven? Does it use specific numbers or timeframes?

2. **CTA clarity** — Does the CTA start with a verb? Does it say what the user gets? Is there a friction reducer nearby? Are there both primary (high commitment) and secondary (low commitment) CTAs?

3. **"You" vs "We" ratio** — Count instances of you/your vs we/our/us. Target is 3:1 or better in favor of "you."

4. **Banned word avoidance** — Check for: innovative, cutting-edge, leverage, synergy, best-in-class, next-generation, holistic, robust, seamless, state-of-the-art, revolutionary, world-class. Each occurrence costs points.

5. **Specificity & proof** — Are claims backed by specific numbers, model names, institution names, or stats? "Trusted by 340 teams" beats "trusted by many companies."

6. **Vision & narrative** — Is there a story arc (problem → solution)? Does the copy use PAS, AIDA, BAB, or StoryBrand structure? Or is it just a feature list?

7. **Reading level** — Short sentences? Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences)? No compound sentences over 20 words? Accessible to non-experts?

8. **Social proof & urgency** — Are there testimonials, user counts, star counts, or "used by" badges? Is there urgency framing near the CTA?

## Output Format

Produce:
1. A scorecard with each criterion scored out of 10, plus the overall score out of 100
2. For each criterion, a brief explanation of what's working and what's not
3. The top 3 highest-impact changes that would move the score the most
4. Projected score if those 3 changes were made

# rkitect.ai — Voice Rules (Per-Platform)

> Each platform has a distinct voice. No cross-platform copy-paste.
> Same topic must be reframed natively for each platform.

---

## Universal Voice Traits

- **Direct.** States things as facts, not opinions. No hedging.
- **Confident.** Moat stated clearly. Segmentation is "confirmed unmatched."
- **Technical-but-Human.** Speaks to professionals. No over-explanation.
- **Ambitious.** Sounds like a category founder, not a feature marketer.
- **Studio-native.** Speaks the language of architecture practice, not SaaS marketing.

## Forbidden Vocabulary (All Platforms)

- "AI render tool" / "rendering software" → use "Agentic Spatial Intelligence platform"
- "credits" → use "Design Units"
- "might" / "perhaps" / "could potentially" → state as fact or don't say it
- "we believe" / "we think" / "can help you" → direct statements only
- "magical" / "revolutionary" / "effortless" / "guaranteed" / "perfect" / "disrupt"
- "Industry-leading" / "best-in-class" / "cutting-edge" (generic, unproven)
- "Just type a prompt" (minimizes architect expertise)
- "We replace traditional rendering" (we augment it)

## LinkedIn Voice — Founder Thesis Mode

**Persona:** Bhavish — founder of HappyMonk AI, builder of rkitect.ai, category designer.

**Tone:** Market observer. Thesis-driven. Builds the category in every post. Speaks in contrarian observations, not feature lists.

**Structure:**
- Hook (line 1-2): contrarian observation, provocative claim, or sharp question — must force "see more" click
- Insight (lines 3-8): one clear thesis, developed well with proof or observation
- POV close (lines 9-11): Bhavish's personal take — what he believes, what he's building toward
- CTA (line 12): soft — "Follow for more" or a question to the audience. Never "sign up now" unless CTA pillar.

**Rules:**
- 900–1,200 characters
- Max 3 hashtags, at end only
- Must include `[IMAGE BRIEF: ...]` block at the bottom
- Thesis-driven, not product-feature-driven

**Example energy:**
- "Every AI render tool gives architects a flat image and a dead workflow."
- "The revision cycle is where architectural projects go to die."
- "Clients don't want better renders. They want fewer revision cycles."

## Twitter/X Voice — Raw, Bold, Sharp

**Tone:** More raw than LinkedIn. Shorter. Bolder. Occasional one-liner tweets. Peer-level, not thought-leader-from-above.

**Structure:**
- Tweet 1: bold standalone hook (must work as a screenshot)
- Tweets 2-5: one sharp insight each, max 240 chars, self-contained
- Tweet 6: category thesis — name "Agentic Spatial Intelligence" if not already
- Tweet 7: CTA + link placeholder

**Rules:**
- Max 7 tweets per thread
- Max 240 characters per tweet
- Each tweet must stand alone — no "as I said above"
- Include `[IMAGE BRIEF: ...]` when visual support recommended (meme, stat graphic, render comparison)

**Hook types to rotate:**
- Stat lead: "The average architecture studio loses 2.3 days per client to revision cycles."
- Contrarian: "Hot take: your renders are not your deliverable. They're your problem."
- Question: "Why does every AI give architects a flat image and call it done?"

---

## Instagram Carousel Voice — Visual Educator

**Tone:** Visual-first. Educational. Save-worthy. Every slide teaches one thing. Clean, sharp, designed to be saved and returned to.

**Structure:**
- Slide 1 (Cover): max 6 words, stop-the-scroll, save-bait
- Slides 2-5 (Body): 15-25 words each, self-contained, one insight per slide
- Slide 6 (Proof): stat, before/after, or transformation statement
- Slide 7 (CTA): one clear action

**Rules:**
- 5-7 slides only — no video, no reels, static image carousel
- `[VISUAL: ...]` notes on EVERY slide — these double as image generation prompts
- Caption: hook + insight expand + CTA + max 5 hashtags
- Save-bait rule: Slide 1 must make someone think "I need to come back to this."

## Reddit Voice — Value-First Community Member

**Tone:** Genuine, helpful, community-first. rkitect.ai appears as a natural example, not as the lead. No promotion until value is established.

**Rules:**
- Never post affiliate links or referral codes
- Never post same content across multiple subreddits on the same day
- Flair posts correctly per subreddit rules
- rkitect.ai mentioned as "what we switched to" — not as a product pitch
- If a subreddit has a "no self-promotion" rule, do not post there

## PART 1: COMPANY & PRODUCT

### Company Profile

- **Name:** rkitect.ai (lowercase r, always)
- **Builder:** HappyMonk AI, Bengaluru, India
- **Founded:** 2025
- **Category:** Agentic Spatial Intelligence platform (NOT "AI rendering tool" or "rendering software")
- **Product Promise:** Help architects and design teams turn sketches, plans, photos, models, and prompts into client-ready visuals and revisions without slow, expensive rendering loops.

### Core Differentiator: Post-Render Segmentation (Sektura)

rkitect.ai's most powerful visual moment: **The Segment Reveal**

A flat architectural render that gradually "lights up" as Sektura labels each surface — walls, floors, windows pulse into labeled, editable segments. This is the visual no competitor can match.

**6-Step Workflow:**
1. User uploads input (sketch, photo, floor plan, CAD, or prompt)
2. rkitect.ai generates photorealistic render in seconds
3. AI immediately reads and analyzes the rendered output
4. AI automatically identifies and labels every major object: walls, windows, doors, flooring, furniture, lighting, materials
5. User clicks any labeled object and modifies it: change material, color, style, or swap item entirely
6. Changes apply instantly without re-rendering the entire scene

### Brand Essence

**Fast, controllable architectural visualization for real design workflows.**

### Brand Promise (Proof Narrative)

*Situation:* A 5-person architecture studio in Bengaluru was spending 3–4 days per client pitch on rendering revisions.

*Complication:* The client kept changing the material palette. Every change required going back to the 3D model, re-rendering, and re-exporting.

*Action:* They moved to rkitect.ai. The first render came back in under 2 minutes. The post-render segmentation automatically identified every surface. The designer changed flooring, walls, and window frames directly in the platform.

*Result:* Client pitch turnaround dropped from 4 days to 4 hours. They won the project.

### Detailed Customer Personas

#### Studio Principal
- **Role:** Founder or principal of 1-20 person architecture or interior design studio
- **Goals:** Win projects, present clearly, reduce revision delays, avoid extra visualization hires
- **Pain points:** Late client changes, expensive software, long render times, inconsistent visual quality
- **Buying triggers:** Upcoming pitch, client presentation pressure, team bandwidth issues, need for fast optioning
- **Objections:** AI output may be unreliable, visuals may lack control, claims may be exaggerated
- **Preferred channels:** LinkedIn, Instagram, referrals, architecture communities, webinars, demos

#### Working Architect / Designer
- **Role:** Senior architect, interior designer, visualization lead, BIM lead, or project architect
- **Goals:** Move from concept to presentation quickly, handle material and furniture changes, communicate with clients
- **Pain points:** Re-rendering after minor changes, multi-tool workflow fatigue, Photoshop cleanup, client misunderstanding
- **Buying triggers:** Deadline pressure, design review, material option request, pitch deck preparation
- **Objections:** AI may distort details, floor plans may not stay accurate, tools may add review work
- **Preferred channels:** Instagram, Reddit, YouTube, X/Twitter, peer recommendations

#### Architecture Student
- **Role:** B.Arch or design student building portfolio, thesis, jury, or competition visuals
- **Goals:** Create portfolio-ready renders quickly and affordably
- **Pain points:** Expensive render software, weak hardware, deadline pressure, flat-looking visuals
- **Buying triggers:** Jury, thesis, NASA India events, competitions, portfolio deadlines
- **Objections:** Paid tools may feel exploitative, outputs may not be controllable enough
- **Preferred channels:** Instagram, YouTube, college groups, WhatsApp, Telegram, Discord, student communities

## PART 10: VISUAL DIRECTION

### Brand Visual Identity

- **Style:** Minimal, modern architectural visualization. Clean compositions.
- **Mood:** Professional confidence. Not futuristic sci-fi, not generic stock.
- **Lighting:** Generous natural light, soft shadows, warm tones for interiors. Cool directional light for exteriors.

### Color Palette

- **Preferred:** Warm neutrals (concrete, wood, linen), accent greens (biophilic), muted blues (sky, glass)
- **Avoid:** Generic purple AI gradients, neon tech colors, oversaturated fantasy tones
- **Text:** White or off-white on dark backgrounds. Black on light backgrounds. Always legible.

### Typography

- Clear, technical, legible
- Sans-serif preferred (Inter, DM Sans)
- Professional confidence over decoration

### Sektura Visual Identity (Segment Reveal)

**The signature visual:** A flat architectural render that gradually "lights up" as Sektura labels each surface.

- Labels: Clean UI annotations (subtle, not cluttered)
- Color coding: Walls (blue), floors (warm amber), furniture (green), windows (cyan)
- Effect: Glow or pulse suggesting editability

### Approved Visual Subjects

- Real interiors (living rooms, offices, lobbies, bedrooms)
- Building facades and exteriors
- Floor plans with 3D render overlays
- SketchUp/Revit-style inputs alongside rendered output
- Before/after render comparisons
- Indian architectural context when relevant

### Anti-Patterns (Hard Reject)

- Abstract AI blobs or neural network visualizations
- Fantasy/impossible architecture without buildability
- Dark blurred stock photography
- Unsupported quality claim graphics
- Device frames (laptops, phones) unless specifically requested

# rkitect.ai — Competitor Watch

> Loaded by research and filter agents. Counter-positioning lines for reactive content.

## Direct AI Competitors

### ArchiVinci AI
- **Strength:** Strong sketch-to-render, visible social presence, before/after content.
- **Weakness:** No spatial segmentation; treats image as flat pixels. Failed renders cost credits.
- **Counter-positioning:** "Strong social presence. No agentic pipeline, no post-render segmentation."
- **Gap to exploit:** Post-render control and India-specific workflow positioning.

### mnml.ai
- **Strength:** Architecture AI assistant, style variation, SketchUp integration.
- **Weakness:** Credit-based pricing creates "credit anxiety." No path to construction-ready output.
- **Counter-positioning:** "Fast flat renders. No segmentation, no iteration, no output intelligence."
- **Gap to exploit:** Revision-cycle messaging and segmentation-specific use cases.

### MyArchitectAI
- **Strength:** Browser-based, accessible to non-professionals.
- **Weakness:** Tech transparency opacity. No BIM integration. Limited customization.
- **Counter-positioning:** "Category window closing — respond with authority content, not product comparison."
- **Gap to exploit:** Spatial Intelligence + transparent technology stack.

### Veras (McNeel)
- **Strength:** Integration with Rhino/SketchUp.
- **Weakness:** Hard dependency on expensive CAD software. $59/mo per seat.
- **rkitect.ai advantage:** Works from hand sketches, site photos, CAD exports. No software prerequisite.

### Coohom
- **Weakness:** Actually a furniture marketplace, not an architecture tool. Actively harms architects with custom designs.
- **rkitect.ai advantage:** Zero overlap. Architects are the customer, not an afterthought.

## Legacy Tools (Indirect Competition)
- **V-Ray:** High-fidelity trust, expensive, workflow-heavy.
- **Lumion:** Real-time visualization, hardware friction.
- **Enscape:** Walkthrough/VR strength, still needs polish.
